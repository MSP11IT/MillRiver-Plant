Sunflower

Helianthus annuus 路 Asteraceae 路 Common Sunflower

Quick Snapshot
Type: Annual flowering plant Origin: North America Season: Summer to early fall Fact: Young sunflowers track the sun, called heliotropism
Description: Sunflowers are tall, bright yellow flowering plants with large blooms and edible seeds. Their sunny faces and seed patterns make them one of the most recognizable garden flowers.

Care Guide

Best conditions for healthy growth

  • Sunlight

    Full sun, 6 to 8 plus hours daily

  • Water

    Moderate, deep watering once or twice weekly

  • Soil

    Loamy or sandy, well draining, compost helps

  • Size

    About 4 to 12 feet tall depending on variety

Natural Habitat

Where it grows in the wild

Habitat

Open meadows, prairies, gardens, and fields

Range

Native to North America and grown worldwide

Human Use

Common uses and cultural notes

Everyday Uses

Seeds, sunflower oil, garden planting, natural dyes

Culture

A symbol of warmth, happiness, and summertime

Wildlife Use

How it supports nature

  • Pollinators

  • Birds

    Seeds feed finches and other seed eating birds
