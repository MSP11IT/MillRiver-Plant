Sugar Maple

Acer saccharum 路 Sapindaceae (Soapberry Family) 路 Sugar Maple

Quick Snapshot
Type: Deciduous Tree Origin: Native to Eastern North America Season: Spring flowers 路 Fall color Interesting fact: Sap flows best in late winter when nights are cold and days are mild.
Description: Sugar maple is a tall hardwood tree known for brilliant fall leaves and sweet sap that can be boiled into maple syrup. It is a classic tree of northeastern forests.

Care Guide

Best conditions for healthy growth

  • Sunlight

    Full sun to partial shade 路 Strongest growth in bright light.

  • Water

    Moderate 路 Keep soil evenly moist when young 路 Drought stress reduces vigor.

  • Soil

    Deep, well drained, fertile soil 路 Slightly acidic is ideal.

  • Size

    Large canopy tree 路 Often 60 to 80 feet tall with age.

Natural Habitat

Where it grows in the wild

Habitat

Mixed hardwood forests, moist slopes, and rich woodland soils.

Range

Eastern Canada and the northeastern United States.

Seasonal Notes

Sap season is late winter to early spring 路 Leaves turn yellow, orange, and red in fall.

Wildlife Use

How it supports nature

  • Pollinators

    Early spring flowers can provide nectar and pollen.

  • Birds

    Seeds and insects in the canopy support woodland birds.

  • Other

    Provides shade, nesting habitat, and leaf litter that enriches soil.

Human Use

Common uses and cultural notes

Everyday Uses

Maple syrup and maple sugar from sap 路 Shade tree 路 Hardwood lumber.

History / Culture

Maple sugaring has deep roots in Indigenous knowledge and regional traditions.

Safety Note

Sap is safe when collected cleanly and boiled properly. Only tap appropriate trees with proper guidance.

Recipe

Try it at home

Maple Syrup on Yogurt and Fruit

A simple way to enjoy maple flavor with a healthy snack.

Ingredients
  • Plain yogurt
  • Fresh fruit
  • Real maple syrup
Steps
  1. Spoon yogurt into a bowl.
  2. Add fruit on top.
  3. Drizzle with maple syrup.

Tip: Start with a small drizzle. Real maple syrup is sweet and flavorful.

Health Benefits

General wellness info

  • Nutrients

    Maple syrup has small amounts of minerals like manganese and zinc.

  • Traditional

    Long used as a seasonal food and energy source in northeastern communities.

  • Caution

    It is still a sugar. Enjoy in moderation.

Fun Facts & Observation

Add something memorable for visitors

What to Look For

Opposite leaf arrangement and classic maple leaf shape. In fall, watch for bright color changes.

Did You Know?

A mature sugar maple can produce many gallons of sap in a good season.
